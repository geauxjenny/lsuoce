Introduction to Contemporary Mathematics

Course Outline

Topic Modules
Counting Techniques & Mathematical Reasoning Modules 1-2

Learn the fundamental counting principle, permutations, combinations, and probability basics. Build the mathematical foundation needed to analyze outcomes and make informed decisions.

Probability & Decision Making Modules 3-5

Explore conditional probability, odds, and events involving "and," "or," and "not." Apply probability concepts to real-world scenarios and everyday decision-making.

Graph Theory & Networks Modules 6-8

Examine graphs, Euler paths, Hamilton circuits, trees, and spanning trees. Learn how mathematical networks can model relationships, routes, and connections.

Data Collection & Statistical Analysis Modules 9-11

Learn how to collect, organize, and analyze data using sampling techniques, frequency distributions, measures of central tendency, and measures of dispersion.

Understanding Data Distributions Module 12

Explore normal distributions, z-scores, percentiles, and the 68–95–99.7 rule. Develop the skills needed to interpret statistical information and evaluate trends.

Linear Programming & Optimization Modules 13-14

Review essential algebra concepts and apply linear programming techniques to solve planning and optimization problems involving resources, constraints, and outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Introduction to Contemporary Mathematics?

Introduction to Contemporary Mathematics is a 3-credit LSU course that explores practical mathematical concepts used to solve real-world problems. Students develop quantitative reasoning skills through topics such as probability, statistics, graph theory, and linear programming.

What topics are covered in the course?

The course covers counting techniques, probability, graph theory, data analysis, statistical measures, normal distributions, and linear programming. Throughout the course, students learn how mathematical concepts can be applied to everyday decision-making and problem-solving.

Is the course self-paced?

Yes. This course features an open-enrollment format that allows students to begin at any time and progress through the coursework at their own pace within the six-month enrollment period.

Are there exams?

Yes. Students complete two proctored examinations: a mid-course exam covering the first half of the course and a final exam covering the remaining modules. These exams assess mastery of the concepts and skills developed throughout the course.

What technology is required?

Students need a computer with high-speed internet access, a webcam with microphone, and either headphones or speakers. Coursework is completed online through Moodle and Pearson MyMathLab, and exams are administered remotely through ProctorU.

Is college credit awarded?

Yes. Successful completion of the course earns 3 LSU credit hours. Course grades are recorded by the LSU Office of the University Registrar and may be applied toward degree requirements, subject to institutional policies.

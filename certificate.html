ABA-Approved in Louisiana

The LSU Paralegal Studies Certificate Program is the only ABA-approved non-credit paralegal program in Louisiana, providing a credential that employers recognize nationwide.

ABA approval means the program meets rigorous national standards for curriculum, faculty, and student services—giving graduates a recognized mark of quality in the legal field.

Paralegal Studies Certificate

Program Curriculum

Students must complete at least nine semester credits of legal specialty courses through synchronous instruction. Transfer up to 10 credit hours from another ABA-approved program may be accepted.

Course Format
Principles of Paralegal Studies Legal specialty · Live-online

An introduction to the paralegal profession covering the American legal system, legal ethics, research sources, interviewing, and document preparation. Introduces substantive areas including bankruptcy, family law, successions and wills, real estate, business organizations, and Louisiana civil procedure.

Legal Research Legal specialty · Live-online

Fundamentals of legal research including primary and secondary sources, computer-assisted research, issue identification, citation, and presentation of findings. Includes Westlaw access for students after passing this course.

Legal Case Analysis Legal specialty · Live-online

Develops skills to read and understand legal opinions with emphasis on legal analysis, key facts, and legal issues. A legal memorandum is assigned as a final project.

Civil Litigation I Legal specialty · Live-online

Overview of Louisiana civil procedural rules. Students learn to locate procedural statutes, draft pleadings, manage docket control, draft discovery documents, and assist in trial preparation from case inception through discovery.

Law Office Technology Legal specialty · Live-online

Practical skills using software commonly found in law offices including Microsoft Office, Adobe Acrobat, TrialDirector, and billing software. Students receive a Legal Technology Certificate from the National Society for Legal Technology upon completion.

The Practicing Paralegal Legal specialty · Live-online

Instruction and practical experience in on-the-job paralegal duties including summarizing depositions, document production, file organization, court filings, and administrative responsibilities such as calendar and docket control.

Paralegal Internship 150 hours · Supervised field experience

A 150-hour supervised internship in a law office, government agency, court, or nonprofit. Full-time workers may request a waiver and take an additional elective instead. Students presently employed as legal assistants may use their employer as a sponsor with program approval.

Legal Ethics Seminar Self-paced · Online

Self-paced online course covering the Louisiana State Bar Association Rules of Professional Conduct, court decisions, and a paralegal's relationship with attorneys, clients, and colleagues. Students must pass a final quiz to receive credit.

Elective Courses 2 required · Choose from updated options

2 electives required. Choose from regularly updated electives reflecting in-demand skills and emerging trends in legal practice.

Customize your learning experience with electives focused on your career goals. Options rotate to reflect current needs in Louisiana legal practice.

Paralegals must work under the supervision of an attorney and may not give independent legal advice, represent clients in court, or settle claims on behalf of clients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this program ABA-approved?

Yes. The LSU Paralegal Studies Certificate Program is the only ABA-approved non-credit paralegal program in Louisiana, providing a credential that employers recognize nationwide.

What are the admission requirements?

Applicants need a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution with a minimum 2.5 overall GPA and 2.5 GPA in English courses, OR 45 college credit hours including at least 18 general education credits with the same GPA requirements. Applicants with three years of legal experience may qualify under exceptional admission with additional documentation.

Can I work while completing the program?

Yes. The program is designed for working professionals with evening live-online classes. Choose the part-time track to pace courses around your schedule, or the full-time track if you can dedicate more time each semester.

Is financial aid available?

This program does not qualify for federal financial aid. Private loan options may be available. Students pay per semester as they register for courses.

Can I transfer credits from another paralegal program?

Applicants who have attended another ABA-approved paralegal studies program can transfer up to 10 credit hours or two non-credit paralegal legal specialty courses, at the program's discretion. No course with a grade lower than a "C" will be accepted.

About This Course

Prepare for smarter financial decisions in entrepreneurship

What You'll Learn

  • Understand and explain the assumptions and principles that underlie Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)
  • Understand and explain the rules of double-entry accounting
  • Calculate the balance of the account
  • Define the steps that make up the accounting cycle
  • Describe the relationship between the major financial statements that make up an annual report
  • Explain the role of finance in the entrepreneurial process
  • Understand the importance of budgeting and financial planning
  • Identify and explain the three major pricing strategies
  • Define and distinguish between fixed and variable costs
  • Calculate the components of a master (static) budget
  • Prepare a cash budget
  • Prepare and analyze pro forma financial statements
  • Develop a basic financial plan
  • Use pro forma financial statements to evaluate a new venture
  • Explain the factors and assumptions involved in break-even analysis
  • Calculate Return on Investment (ROI) and payback period

Accounting and Finance for Entrepreneurs

Course Outline

Topic Time
Financial Accounting Fundamentals 1 hr

Learn the core principles of accounting, including GAAP, double-entry accounting, account balances, and the accounting cycle. Understand how financial information is recorded and organized.

Reading Financial Statements 45 min

Explore the relationship between income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements. Learn how to interpret financial reports and use them to evaluate business performance.

Finance for Entrepreneurs 45 min

Examine the role of finance in launching and growing a business. Understand how entrepreneurs use financial information to make strategic decisions and assess opportunities.

Budgeting and Financial Planning 45 min

Develop practical budgeting skills, including cash budgets, master budgets, and financial planning techniques that support business growth and sustainability.

Pricing and Cost Analysis 45 min

Learn how pricing strategies, fixed costs, and variable costs impact profitability. Apply financial concepts to evaluate products, services, and business models.

Forecasting and Pro Forma Statements 30 min

Create financial projections and pro forma statements to evaluate new ventures, model future performance, and support decision-making.

Break-Even and Investment Analysis 30 min

Calculate break-even points, return on investment (ROI), and payback periods to assess business opportunities and financial viability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your refund policy?

You may request a refund up to 7 days from the purchase date. The registration fee will only be refunded if less than 10% of the course has been completed. Completion percentage can be viewed on the Course Progress page from within the course.

What are the admission requirements?

Applicants need a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution with a minimum 2.5 overall GPA and 2.5 GPA in English courses, OR 45 college credit hours including at least 18 general education credits with the same GPA requirements. Applicants with three years of legal experience may qualify under exceptional admission with additional documentation.

Can I work while completing the program?

Yes. The program is designed for working professionals with evening live-online classes. Choose the part-time track to pace courses around your schedule, or the full-time track if you can dedicate more time each semester.

Is financial aid available?

This program does not qualify for federal financial aid. Private loan options may be available. Students pay per semester as they register for courses.

Can I transfer credits from another paralegal program?

Applicants who have attended another ABA-approved paralegal studies program can transfer up to 10 credit hours or two non-credit paralegal legal specialty courses, at the program's discretion. No course with a grade lower than a "C" will be accepted.
